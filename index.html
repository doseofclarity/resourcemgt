Overview: Resource Management Concepts

Definition:

Resource management in nursing involves the efficient and effective deployment of financial, human, material, and time resources to achieve optimal patient outcomes and organizational goals.

Key principles (Pillitteri):

  • Stewardship & accountability
  • Cost containment without compromising quality
  • Evidence‑based staffing models
  • Just‑in‑time inventory for supplies

Typical nursing department resource allocation

Primary resource in any healthcare setting

Human resources – skilled nursing staff are the most critical asset.

Human Resource Management

Nurse Staffing Models

  • Acuity‑based staffing
  • Primary nursing vs team nursing
  • Magnet recognition and shared governance
  • Nurse‑patient ratios: California law 1:5 med‑surg, maternity 1:4

Staff Retention Strategies

  • Mentorship & residency programs
  • Competitive compensation & benefits
  • Work‑life balance / self‑scheduling
  • Professional development ladder

High nursing turnover increases cost by 1.3–1.5× annual salary per nurse.

Material & Supply Management

Inventory control systems

  • Just‑in‑time (JIT) – reduces holding costs
  • Par‑level systems for nursing units
  • Automated dispensing cabinets (Pyxis)
  • ABC analysis (high‑value vs high‑volume)

Waste reduction strategies

  • Lean methodology (5S)
  • Green nursing: reusable vs single‑use
  • Expiration date tracking software
Most wasted supply in maternity units
Unused sterile gloves and opened but unused catheter kits – up to 12% waste

Nursing Role & Leadership

  • Participate in budget planning and cost‑saving initiatives
  • Advocate for safe staffing ratios through shared governance
  • Implement waste reduction programs (e.g., green OR, supply standardization)
  • Use evidence‑based management to improve resource utilization
  • Mentor new nurses in time management and prioritization

Pillitteri MCN Focus:

Maternal‑child units require high acuity resources – nurse managers must balance patient safety, family satisfaction, and cost efficiency.
